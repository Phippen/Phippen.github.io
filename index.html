Singing Web App

This project was created for COM S 309 in my junior year. The project was to create a browser application that listened to the user's voice as they sang a song and awarded them points based on the expected pitch for the note that they were singing. The app also featured a keyboard mode where the user could use their computer keyboard to play musical notes along to a predetermined song. My team consisted of four members, my responsibilities during the project were establishing the connection between our MySQL database and our web application using a Hibernate framework.

Voice-a-Sketch

This project was developed during HackISU in the fall of 2015. Using Google Polymer as a template, we created a browser application that displayed an HTML canvas that continued to draw a line while listening for voice commands to change direction, stop, and start again. My involvement in this project was focused on implementing the features of Google Polymer and researching voice recognition.

Cumulative Reflection

My education at ISU started as a very individual experience. Classes were designed to test our logic and problem solving and then teach us how to solve those problems more effectively on our own. My first two years at ISU prepared me to write efficient code and be a competent programmer on my own.

These last two years have been the other side of the coin, classes require multiple group projects and expect planning and analyzing to come into the projects. These classes have taught me to think critically about what a client wants, design and plan my approach before coding, and then communicate with others as a team to complete the project, rather than bash it out on my own.
